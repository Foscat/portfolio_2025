Daimler Trucking Hackathon

July 19th-21st 2019

Overview

The Daimler Trucking Hackathon of 2019 marked a pivotal moment in my journey as a developer. Freshly graduated from my second bootcamp, I attended the event with a friend, initially just for fun. Little did we know, we would be tasked with revolutionizing the repair process for the company. Despite being overlooked by senior teams, our group of junior developers, including my friend and I, banded together to tackle the challenge head-on.

Against all odds, our collaborative efforts led to a resounding victory, showcasing our ability to deliver practical solutions tailored to the client's actual needs. Inspired by our success, some of us pooled our winnings to embark on an ambitious endeavor - starting a company to develop the app for Daimler. As the sole developer who joined in, I faced immense challenges, teaching myself React Native to transform the web-based solution into a mobile app. However, despite our best efforts, the venture ultimately faltered due to the actions of our project leader, who engaged in shady backroom deals by signing contracts under his own company rather than the one we established with our winnings.

Hackathon App (Reinforce)

The problem

Help the company technicians in repair and maitinance work along with ways to track process speed. This will allow the company to understand when and where slowdowns happen. This system needs to be hands free as possible and should not require the technician to interact with a screen. They also wanted a way for technicians that compleated work fater than the average time to give feedback on how they did it.

The solution

To address the challenge presented by Daimler, our team leveraged a combination of innovative technologies and collaborative expertise. We began by utilizing my "MERN App Template" as the foundation for the application, taking advantage of its backend capabilities and pre-built sign-in process. With this solid groundwork in place, each member of the team focused on a specific aspect of the project. One teammate developed an .xml parser to efficiently read instruction files, while another dedicated their efforts to creating an AWS Lambda function to handle inputs and step positions. Meanwhile, my friend and I tackled the crucial task of implementing voice interactions with the client. This involved designing a seamless process for technicians to issue commands vocally, transmitting them to the Lambda function, and seamlessly integrating the response back into the application. Additionally, we worked on converting the text of the current step from the parser into speech, ensuring a fluid and intuitive user experience throughout the repair process.

The outcome

The app developed during the Daimler Trucking Hackathon provided a revolutionary solution to the company's repair and maintenance workflow. By leveraging an automated voice system, technicians could efficiently access truck information and receive step-by-step directions without the need for manual interaction with a screen. This hands-free approach not only improved process speed tracking but also enhanced technician productivity and satisfaction. Additionally, the system allowed technicians to provide valuable feedback on their work, enabling continuous improvement and optimization of repair procedures. The app's success at the hackathon demonstrated its potential to streamline operations and drive efficiency within Daimler's repair process.

Post Hackathon Work (Tech Assist)

Transition

After the hackathon we made a company and named it "Reinforce" after our team name from the hackathon. We also changed the name of the app to "Tech Assist" to better represent it as a product of our company. I got a co-working space since I did not have a home office at the time to develop the app. The client gave us some .xml files with some work instructions to use for development and had many changes they wanted to make. Primaraliy the desire to make it a moblie app rather than a website. I was honsetly in way over my head but I was determined to make it work.

Development & Challenges

After the hackathon and the establishment of our new company, we engaged in several meetings with Daimler to delineate the project's scope and objectives. Participating in these discussions was an invaluable experience for me, as a relatively inexperienced developer at the time. We covered a wide range of topics, including feature requirements, implementation strategies, timelines, and budget considerations, laying the groundwork for the professional development of the application.

Given the requirement for a mobile version of the app and the ambiguity surrounding the device platform, I decided to initiate development using React Native. Leveraging my prior experience with React.js, I swiftly adapted to React Native, allowing us to swiftly create a rudimentary prototype compatible with both major platforms.

However, transitioning to mobile development presented its own set of challenges. I needed to migrate the parsing logic to a server and develop a micro-service to handle it. Additionally, I faced the task of enhancing the parsing logic to accommodate the intricacies of the XML documents. Originally written in Python and notably complex, I reverse engineered the logic in JavaScript and added the necessary complexity to meet our requirements. I did this conversion in two weeks, where I also successfully expanded the capabilities of the parsing logic, enabling users to access over 500 sets of instructions for 5 different trucks solely through voice prompts.

As an example of the logic flow, consider the scenario where a technician needs to run a [Service_Type] on a [Model_Number]. The program first identifies all documents associated with the specified model number, filters them to locate the document number of the requested service type, and then sends the corresponding XML file to the parser micro-service. Once parsed, the instructions are returned to the app as an array of objects, with each object containing the step instructions as a string and an array of sub-steps if applicable, ready to be read aloud to the user.

Outcome

Despite the promising start following the hackathon victory, the post-hackathon journey ultimately ended in disappointment and frustration. While we made significant progress in defining project requirements, developing a prototype, and laying the groundwork for further development, unforeseen challenges emerged that derailed our efforts.

Regrettably, the project's downfall stemmed from the actions of our team leader, who made unilateral decisions without consulting the rest of the team. In a disheartening turn of events, it was discovered that the team leader had signed contracts with Daimler under a company he owned, sidelining the rest of the team and denying us the opportunity to continue our work on the project. Despite my best efforts to salvage the situation, I found myself cut out of the project entirely, lacking the resources and leverage to rectify the situation.

While the outcome was disappointing, the experience served as a valuable lesson in the importance of transparency, communication, and trust within a team dynamic. Although the project did not materialize as intended, the lessons learned and the skills acquired during the post-hackathon work have undoubtedly contributed to my growth as a developer and have prepared me for future challenges and opportunities.
